# Last Mile Margin Demo Video Plan

## Goal

Create a short, clear product demo that shows contractors how Last Mile Margin saves time by turning messy daily information into organized profit, claim, contract, and route decisions.

The video should feel practical, not salesy. The viewer should think: "I could actually use this during the day."

## Main Demo: 90 Seconds

### 0:00-0:08 - Hook

Screen: Dashboard

Voiceover:
"Final-mile contractors do not have time to manually chase every claim, route cost, and contract detail. Last Mile Margin keeps the day organized in one place."

On-screen action:
- Start on Dashboard.
- Let the viewer see Day Net Profit, Claims, Revenue, Costs, and Needs Attention.

### 0:08-0:22 - Dashboard Value

Screen: Dashboard

Voiceover:
"The dashboard shows what happened today: profit, claims exposure, revenue, costs, margin, and the items that need attention."

On-screen action:
- Click one dashboard number or attention item if clickable.
- Show it taking the user to the related page.
- Return to Dashboard or continue to Intake.

### 0:22-0:40 - AI Intake

Screen: Intake

Voiceover:
"Instead of typing everything by hand, drop in an email, screenshot, PDF, route sheet, contract terms, or notes. The system pulls out what matters."

On-screen action:
- Click Intake in the sidebar.
- Paste a short claim-style email or route note.
- Click Analyze Intake.
- Show extracted information fields.

Sample text to paste:
```
Customer reported wall damage during delivery. Route 14, Stop 23.
Driver Mike Johnson. Lowe's Store #1234. Claim amount $950.
Photos attached. Delivery date May 31, 2026.
```

### 0:40-0:52 - Send Intake Where It Belongs

Screen: Intake

Voiceover:
"After review, send the draft directly where it belongs: Claims, Contracts, Profitability, or the saved day."

On-screen action:
- Click Save to Claim.
- Show the confirmation.
- Click Open or go to Claims.

### 0:52-1:05 - Claims Board

Screen: Claims

Voiceover:
"Claims stay simple. Drag a claim from review to in progress or resolved, and the status updates automatically."

On-screen action:
- Show the claims board.
- Drag one claim card from Needs Review to In Progress.
- Drag another to Resolved if it looks smooth.

### 1:05-1:18 - Route Profit Check

Screen: Profitability > Route Profit Check

Voiceover:
"Before running a route, check whether it is worth it. Pick the contract, turn on the pay types that apply, add custom charges, and see profit update live."

On-screen action:
- Click Profitability.
- Select Route Profit Check.
- Pick Lowe's or another contract.
- Toggle one pay type.
- Add a custom charge like "Stairs Fee" for 75.
- Click Revenue or Labor card to show the contextual popup.
- Click outside the popup to close it.

### 1:18-1:28 - Contracts Roll-Up

Screen: Profitability > All Contracts

Voiceover:
"At the contract level, click any row to review the numbers behind revenue, costs, claims, net profit, and margin."

On-screen action:
- Switch to All Contracts.
- Click a contract row.
- Show the premium popup editor.

### 1:28-1:35 - Save Day

Screen: Top bar / Dashboard

Voiceover:
"At the end of the day, save the day. You can come back later and see exactly what happened."

On-screen action:
- Click Save Day.
- Open Saved Days briefly.

### 1:35-1:40 - Closing

Screen: Dashboard or Intake

Voiceover:
"Last Mile Margin helps contractors capture the messy stuff once, understand the numbers, and keep moving."

On-screen action:
- End on Dashboard or Intake.

## Recording Setup

Use a browser window around 1440 by 900 if possible. This gives enough room for the sidebar, top controls, cards, and popups without crowding.

Use dark mode for the main demo. It feels more premium and shows the blue/green/red dashboard numbers clearly.

Use a clean demo account with a few realistic sample items:
- 3 to 4 claims
- 4 contracts
- 1 saved day
- A few route pay types enabled
- 1 custom charge already created, plus add one during the recording

Keep the mouse movement slow and intentional. Do not click too fast. Let each screen breathe for one second after major actions.

## Best Opening Line

"Final-mile contractors do not need another spreadsheet. They need one place to drop the messy stuff, see what matters, and keep moving."

## Best Closing Line

"Last Mile Margin turns claims, contracts, routes, and daily profit into something you can actually act on."

## Notes For Editing

Keep the main video under 100 seconds.

Add simple text callouts only where they help:
- "AI Intake"
- "Drag to update status"
- "Contract-specific pay types"
- "Custom charge"
- "Save Day"

Do not show every page. Show the workflow.

Do not explain every number. Explain why the number matters.

Use a calm, confident voiceover. The software should feel useful, not flashy.
